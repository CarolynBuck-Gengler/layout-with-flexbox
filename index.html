Hi, I'm Carolyn.

I am Web Developer with a background in Cognitive Psychology and programming experiments. Like many I'm having to make the best of it during the COVID-19 pandemic.
One thing that has drawn my interest lately is jsPsych, a Javascript library explicitly developed to help psychology researchers put some of their experiments online. I hope to maybe someday combine my love of programming and developing websites with my research and experiment background by writing online experiments using jsPsych.
But in the meantime...

During the pandemic, one thing our family has really gotten into is bicycling. It started in March when we went for a walk every day, for exercise and to take a break. We started with short walks but soon built it up to an hour. When the weather got warm enough, we swapped our walking shoes for our bicycles, because we could go so much farther! Now we get in an hour-long ride 4 to 5 times each week, trying to get to different parts of our city; on Sundays we go nearly 2 hours. We always go early in the morning to avoid the heat later in the day. It's been a wonderful new family activity!

Another thing I've taken up during this time is baking, especially bread. I was never a bread baker before, but I found recipes that didn't require kneading, and the bread is baked in my Dutch oven. I've been working with one main recipe, trying to perfect my execution of it, and then branching out with greater proportions of whole wheat flour, and even adding herbs and seeds. I'm also trying other recipes, such as a dill-cottage cheese bread and a honey whole wheat bread. I even got myself a new smaller Dutch oven. It seems like a lot of people are doing this, because some ingredients (e.g., flour and yeast) have been hard to find at times; I still haven't been able to get vital wheat gluten. If this keeps up, I'll have to open a bakery!

Lastly, in the evenings we've been binging a lot of movies and TV shows we missed in the past. We got Disney+ and CBS All Access to see The Mandelorian, Clone Wars, Picard, Star Trek: Discovery, and Star Trek: Enterprise; and a couple weeks ago were thrilled to be able to watch Hamilton. We also watched several of the Andrew Lloyd Weber musicals (including Cats and Phantom of the Opera) when they were aired, and even some ballet versions of Romeo and Juliet. Now we are catching up on Star Trek: Next Generation and Deep Space Nine.
